Introduction

This webpage is made to be the part of the final exam project for the course 02805, Social Graphs and Interactions 2021 Fall, at the Technical University of Denmark.  Please see the menu above to navigate and see an overview of or analysis, to download our explainer notebook or the datasets we have worked with or to contact us for further questions.

Motivati​on

Our team has chosen to work with the world of Rick and Morty the famous animated science fiction sitcom.
 
Rick and Morty is a series that follows a genius alcoholic and careless scientist, who drags his grandson Morty on insanely dangerous adventures across the universe. Rick Sanchez is living with his daughter Beth’s family and constantly bringing her, his son-in-law Jerry, granddaughter Summer, and grandson Morty into intergalactic adventures.

The idea behind choosing this specific series is that it has many different dimensions and locations giving a really good base to discover networks and communities in an universe that doesn’t make a lot of sense at a first glance. The main appeal to the series are the dimensional interferences. The questionable continuity and randomness makes it incredibly interesting for us to analyze. We know that there is no plot in Rick and Morty (“This story just makes no sense. It’s a story about not having a story,” said Dan Harmon) Then what are the underlying connections and so in a no-sense universe? We are sure we can make it make sense by analyzing it, building its networks, creating graphs, identifying communities, well-linked characters and analyzing the sentiment of different characters, communities, even episodes or seasons. We also try to compare it with evaluations given on IMDB and draw our own conclusions by understanding our own analysis.

Word clouds for main characters and their sentiment analysis


These are the most used bigrams per character. We have found some particularly interesting ones, like "w w" as Morty's second most used bigram. The reason for that is his stutter and the fact that most of his sentences start with question words (like what, who, where, when, why etc.). Almost all characters have 'oh god' in their top bigrams, and we can also make an interesting observiation on who they often talk to - just take a look at Summer's and Beth's top bigrams. We have also discovered that some of the bigrams (hungry apple) are very specific to one episode - the characters just have said them so many times they have made it to be their most common word combinations.
